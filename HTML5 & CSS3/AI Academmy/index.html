Why should you become an artificial intelligence professional?

The market for professionals with expertise in artificial intelligence continues to grow. In February, the UN reported that patent application numbers for innovations based on artificial intelligence had increased exponentially in recent years.

The study of artificial intelligence began in the 1950s at Carnegie Mellon University in the USA. Since then, a lot has changed. At the time, the goal of the pioneering researchers, Herbert Simon, Allen Newell and John McCarthy, was to create a “being” that simulated a human life.

Today, artificial intelligence underpins the automation of many businesses, with software that learns to make the best decisions by analyzing the data generated from previous decisions.

In practice, artificial intelligence already inhabits a wide spectrum of our routines. Whether it's the music recommendation on Spotify or the best way to work through Waze.

But for those who want to work in the area, where to start? After all, knowledge in artificial intelligence is very broad and required in positions ranging from Data Scientist to Aerospace Engineer. Those with the skills to analyze, organize and translate bits of digital information into meaningful human experiences are sure to find a career in artificial intelligence a rewarding opportunity.
